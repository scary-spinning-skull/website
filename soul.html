SOUL POINTS

Soul is an abstraction of a character's courage and their ability to "push through" and succeed despite the odds. All characters begin the game with five Soul points, but the exact number varies throughout the couse of a session as they have several uses:

  • Modifying the outcome of a Story die roll
  • Giving the character access to higher tiers of spells
  • Determining the ability of a character to overcome his fear

Story Dice
One Soul point can be spent to modify any one Ability die roll. If the player wishes to affect both Ability dice rolls, they must spend two soul points. The player must decide whether or how to spend the point before casting their dice. They may decide to spend a point on their second roll, lowest roll, a marginal loss, or whatever other criterion the DM will agree to. However, once the Soul point has been committed, it can't been taken back, regardless of the outcome.

For example, a player chooses to spend a Soul point on a rat's leap across a chasm. He decides that it will be used to push a low Ability roll over the difficulty roll if there are any marginal failures. He rolls 2/7-4/6, neigher of which meet that criterion. The Soul point is lost, as is the rat, who plummets to his death.

Spells
Spells are divided into levels reflecting increasing demands on the psyche of the caster. Players spend one point to cast a level 1 spell, two points for level 2, and so on. The expenditure of these points do not affect the outcome of the dice rolls; a player must spend additional Soul points to enhance the subsequent Ability rolls.

Courage
Substreet inhabitants can sometimes react poorly to surprise - some freeze or flee. The Soul level of the character is important for determining a character's reaction to a frightening situation such as an unexpected encounter with a human or cat. Players must take the character's Soul score, double it, and roll a d10. If the doubled Soul score is higher, the character keeps his wits about him. If the Soul and d10 are equal (like a Complicated challenge roll), the character freezes in place before mustering his courage on the next turn. Those who fail the roll run off and cower if they cannot.

For example, say that a Mischief of three rats turns a corner in an alley and encounters a hungry Cat. Rat 1 has a current Soul score of 3, meaning that he has to roll below 6 with a d10 to keep his wits about him and deploy the can of mace he has strapped to his back. He succeeds: 6/4. Rat 2 is emotionally exhausted because he cast a very important spell earlier in the session, and his Soul has been reduced to 1. He fails his roll dramatically: 2/7. He runs away, while Rat 3, with 4 Soul points, rolls 8/8 and is rooted to the ground. On the next turn he rolls 8/4, allowing him to gather his courage and join Rat 1 in his fight with the now-blinded cat.

Gaining Soul

A character's Soul score can be improved in various ways.

Permanent
Characters have the option of increasing either their Soul or their hit points (HP) by 1 per level. Soul points are otherwise immutable outside of very rare circumstances (such as may occur during missions, perhaps?).
Temporary
There are various potions which can be purchased or brewed by a character who is an Alchemist. Humans have various concoctions that a cautious denizen of the Substreet can use, with strange names like Adderall and PCP. These concoctions are sufficient for one hour and are subject to the same rules governing use of potions.

Particularly brave characters may seek out the devices and lab equipment used by the humans who created intelligent animals. Some are known to vastly increase the natural abilities of a user, although such a quest is perilous.
