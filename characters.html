Characters

Species
Decades of experimentation in Human laboratories and subsequent hybridization have created six races of intelligent animals.

Mice
Mice, or meece, are the smallest of the Substreet races. This puts enemies at a Disadvantage when trying to attack them for the first two rounds of a combat encounter. They are also the lightest and quietest of the Substreet species and gain an Advantage for the first attempt to squeeze through a tight space.

Lab Rats
Lab Rats are the most versatile of the Substreet races. Different lineages have been bred by Humans for different purposes for untold generations. They are sicklier than other races and only begin with 4 HP, but their heightened intelligence gives them +1 to their caster level (up to 3) and one extra field of Academic expertise.

Sewer Rats
Sewer Rats are hybrids of Lab Rats and unintelligent wild rats. They are less psychologically stable than the other races as they are not solely the product of guided evolution and genetic engineering. Their great strength lends them a +1 to their Fighter score (up to 4) but their Caster score is limited to 1.
Salamanders
Salamanders are a species that is most often bred for their healing properties. They heal at twice the rate of other races and can even regrow lost limbs in three months. Salamanders retain the positive effects from potions for twice as long as other races, but they are sensitive to humidity and will weaken if they are in a dry environment for more than an hour (e.g. a Human structure with climate control), forcing their players to make their Fighter and Dodger Ability rolls at a Disadvantage.

Cats
Cats are sometimes used in Human experiments before escaping into the wild or being released by deranged "cat people". There is a 30% chance that any cat encountered by player characters in the wild will be an intelligent Cat. Such Cats are extremely dangerous as they loathe all other intelligent animal species. Both Dodger and Caster ability checks are performed with advantage for the first two rounds. They are not actually Substreet denizens as they do not go underground, and some of them even live with Humans! Cats cannot be played as PCs.

Slime Mold
All intelligent Slime Molds descend from a single half ounce sample of an ill-concieved attempt to use funguses to cultivate organoids - specifically, organoids composed of human brain cells. Slime Molds are hideous and only able to craw slowly across surfaces. As Slime Molds are essentially all clones of each other, they have the same Ability scores: 0+double disadvantage for everything but Magus, which is fixed at 3 with advantage for the first roll. Slime Molds cannot be played as PCs.

Abilities
A character's Abilities reflects their broad range of skills in certain types of activity. There are five types of Ability which cannot be altered under normal circumstances:

  • Fighter - Melee combat and tests of strength
  • Caster - Magic and feats of the mind
  • Dodger - Dexterity and speed
  • Orator - Persuading people
  • Scalawag - Trickery and financial crimes

Players making a character must distribute the following bonuses: +3, +2 (2x), +1 (2x). For example, player wishing to make a trader may choose: Fighter +1, Caster +1, Dodger +2, Orator +3, Scalawag +2. These bonuses are applied to the d6 of a Challenge roll.

Say that this trader is attempting to talk a town guard into letting him through. She makes a Challenge roll and gets 4/6-5/6. With the bonus, however, this becomes 7/6-8/6, turning the failure into a success.


Expertise
Characters have three areas of Expertise when they begin: martial, academic, and professional. Martial expertise refers to skills in fighting in certain situations, while academic expertise refers to specific areas of knowledge. Professional expertise refers to knowledge related to more concrete matters, various skills and crafts. When a character's action falls within the domain of his area of expertise, the player's first roll is advantaged. Players who want to expand a character's repetoirs must spend a certain amount of XP, then give the character two months of study.

Health
All characters begin with 5 hit points (HP), an abstract indicator of a person's overall condition. When a character gains a level, the player may choose to increase their health by 1 HP or gain an additional Soul point. If a character loses all of their HP, they are not necessarily dead, merely too wounded or ill to continue fighting; to determine what their actual condition is, the owner of the character must make an unmodified Challenge roll after combat, or whatever event has harmed the character, has ceased. If they have died, a spell such as Prevent Death can still be used immediately to revive them.

Morale

Morale is determined by a character's Soul.
