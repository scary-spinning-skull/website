SPELL DESCRIPTIONS



Believe Lie

The target of this spell will believe any lie, but not a direct command, told to them by the caster in the next fifteen seconds. The spell lasts for a full minute, after which they will come to their senses and realize that the lie is untrue. They will also remember who told them the lie. The target will otherwise behave normally while under this spell. Miscasting this spell will cause the caster to believe the lie instead, which may trigger bizarre behavior. The caster may attempt to perform other actions while so affected, but they will be double Hindered

For example, Scabbers the Mouse has, for some reason, attempted to make a rat believe that she, too, is a mouse. The spell backfires, and Scabbers becomes consumed with the idea, shouting "I am a mouse!" and demanding that people nearby agree with him.

Betrify

This spell embetters the target, causing their next action to be Emboldened, even if the action will be Emboldened by some other effect. The caster cannot use this spell on himself, and the target of this spell cannot be enbettered again for 24 hours. A miscasting of this spell will cause the next action of the target and the caster to be Hindered instead, although neither knows it until one of them attempts some action.

Buzz

This spell causes the target to hear the sound of several large flies buzzing hear their ears for up to five minutes. This noise is very distracting, and as a result all of the target's actions are Hindered for its duration. A failure causes the target to hear the voices of their friends cheering them on, and their next action is Emboldened instead.

Explode Head

This spell causes the target's head to explode with the force of one stick of dynamite. No one near the target is harmed by the explosion, although they can see and hear it. A failure causes the caster to explode with the same force. Casting this spell requires the expenditure of six Soul points and reduces the caster's HP to 1.

Distraction

The target of this spell will be distracted by something which may or may not be real for up to a minute. While under the influence of this spell, the target may still look at the caster, but they will not act against them.

For example, Scabbers has once again tried to cast a spell, this time successfully. The target, a guard, becomes consumed by the delusion that his commander has been stealing cheese from his locker and he begins honing his sword in anticipation of confronting him about it later. Scabbers now has a full minute to escape from the cell before his guard remembers that he does not keep cheese in his locker.

ESP

Only the caster may be affected by this spell, which enables him to magically sense one thing up to 10' away for up to a full minute, during which time they are still able to perform Hindered acts. This thing may have happened up to a day in the past or up to a minute in the future, and the spell allows them to use only one sense. The sense used will be highly specific, so the caster may detect proprioreception or balance in a target area as if they were in that space, and they also access senses which they do not normally possess, such as magnetoreception. Miscasting this spell will cause the caster to use a random sense somewhere other than the intended target.

For example, Scabbers now has to find his way out of the jail. He attempts to use ESP to see into the next room, but he critically fails, and for the next ten minutes he can feel infrared heat as if he were standing in the corner of his prison cell at the same time as he was being thrown into it.

Forget Ensorcellment

The target of this spell is made unaware that he has been affected by a spell of any kind. He will rationalize the effects of the spell itself in any way he can, becoming confused and irate if those rationalizations are questioned. This is a very important spell to use in non-combat situations where the target may be on your side. Failure causes the caster to forget one randomly selected spell for one month.

For example, a mouse is half starved after being hit with a super effective No Opening spell and spending a day in a small closet. When the spell fades and he stumbles out, he decides that he must have forgotten to look in the door's direction while searching for an exit.

Gigachad

The target of this spell becomes more respected as he develops a strong jaw, cheekbones, and a human-like chin. The fur on their head grows longer and thicker and looks as if it has been combed back. Salamanders grow a head of hair which behaves in the same way. The spell lasts for 24 hours, and a failure causes the caster to seem weak and uninteresting to others for two weeks.

Prevent Death

Casters can only use this spell if someone else, be they PC, NPC, or even an animal, has just been killed. The target's body will heal enough to allow them to be brought to 1 HP if aid is administered quickly; otherwise the target dies permanently. This spell will not work if the body is not intact, such as after being dissolved in acid or torn in half by an opposum. Targets who have been dead for more than a minute will not be revived. A miscasting of this spell will reduce the caster's HP to 1.

Reverse Handedness

The target of this spell has their handedness reversed for an hour: a left-handed target becomes right-handed and vice versa. A target in combat suddenly becomes clumsy as he is now holding his weapon in the wrong hand. He must spend a turn switching hands to use his weapons "correctly" or be Hindered as he fights. Miscasting this spell makes the target ambidexterous, and his next action is Emboldened.

Sleepy

Targets become sleepy for fifteen seconds. If they are performing an action, it will now be Hindered. If the target is already prone or in a relaxed pose, they will fall into a natural sleep from which they can be woken by a loud noise or being shaken by someone. The caster passes out if this spell backfires and he must be shaken by someone else to wake him.

Throw Voice

This spell allows the caster to project his voice to any point within his line of sight. This spell is limited to sounds that the caster can produce vocally, from a loud shout to a quiet whisper. The caster cannot throw his voice in a language he doesn't know unless he is mimicking something he has memorized. A miscasting of this spell will cause an enemy near the target to hear blistering insults shouted in the target's voice.

On The Wall

Casting this spell causes text to appear on any vertical surface for up to ten minutes. The text can be between 1'' and 1' high and fill a space of up to one square yard. If the caster is illiterate, the "text" resembles asemic writingf. A failure produces text that tell readers exactly where the caster is hiding and how to catch them (even if the caster is illiterate!).

Understand Languages

The target of this spell can understand any spoken language for ten minutes. They cannot speak it themselves or read it, though, even if they are literate in other languages. In the event of a failure, the caster will only be able to utter word salad for an hour.
