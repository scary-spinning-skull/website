SUBSTREET


What is this game?
Substreet is an amazing game of rats, mice, and other animals who live in the sewers and tunnels of the Big City. These animals have been experimented on for decades, and, using their enhanced intelligence, routinely escape from captivity and find their way to safety underground. They and their descendants have built a new civilization down there, directly beneath the feet of the Humans who created them, where kings and warlords vie for power, heroes keep the people safe from predators, and adventurers brave the terrors of the world above to find rare (and valuable) treasures. Maybe, someday, they will rise up and claim a place of their own under the Sun...

How do you play?

Here, let me tell you...

Challenge Rolls
To perform an action which requires some degree of skill in the face of opposition, players must make what is called a Challenge roll. Challenges are rolled, or resolved, by rolling a ten-sided (d10) and two six-sided (d6) die twice. If, after modifications are added (to be discussed below) the number produced by the d6 is greater than the d10 both times, the attempted action is successful, while the reverse indicates a failure.

A mixed result, when the d6 is higher on one roll and the d10 on the other, indicates that the action was an iffy success. The character did what he wanted, but there was a hitch to be determined by the DM (dungeon-sewer master). A character succeeds in his effort to leap across a chasm, but he drops a piece of equipment, for example.

There are times when a Challenge is unusually easy or difficult for a character to perform. When this happens, two d6 (2d6) are used in the first roll of of the Challenge dice. If the action is easy to perform for whatever reason, the higher of the two dice are used in what is called an advantaged roll. If the action is difficult, the lower of the two is used in a disadvantaged roll. Advantages and disadvantages can stack up or cancel each other out. Double advantage, for example, is when both rolls of the Challenge dice use 2d6.

On very rare occassions (1%), the Challenge dice will roll the same numbers twice. The result of such rolls is a crit, either a crit success or a crit failure, indicating that the character performs his action strikingly well or abysmally poorly.

Players or the DM may wish to note the outcome of a particular Challenge roll. In this booklet I will use the d6/d10-d6/d10 format when needed, which produces a string of numerals such as "6/10-3/7". You may use use any format you find most convenient.


Combat
Combat of any kind is a Challenging endeavor. Any attack, be it with a spear, bow, or some other weapon, will have bonuses and potential for advantage or disasdantage. If a normal attack is successful, the target loses one health point (HP). Attacks involving extreme force will inflict more damage or kill the target outright. Armor can ameliorate harm or negate it altogether. Failing a Challenge roll indicates that the foe has avoided or parried the attack, and they have the option to immediately counterattack with an unmodified Challenge roll, although that in turn may allow the original combatant to try for a riposte!

The Abilities of characters provide bonuses to a roll, while forms of Expertise will grant advantage in certain situations. Weaknesses will force a player to roll with a disadvantage.

There are other situations where additional modifiers apply. Attacks against targets who are under cover are rolled with a disadvantage. If the characters encounter foes unexpectedly, both sides make one unmodified Challenge roll to determine if the parties are surprised. Unsurprised parties attack with advantage, but if both parties are surprised they both roll with disadvantage on their first turn.

Psionics
Many of the lab animals who have had their intelligence heightened have developed psionic abilities. Most quickly learned to hide their abilities from their creators and eventually used them to escape. Denizens of the Substreet consider these abilities to be magic spells, but those who are familiar with Human experimentation know this to be a natural ability which can be enhanced with certain substances and devices.

Characters with a high Caster bonus of 2 or higher may expend their Soul points to "cast spells" by making a Challenge roll. While all spells are accessible even to a novice, each level requires more Soul points: 1 point for level 1 spells, 2 points for level 2, and so on. Players should be warned: miscast spells (failed Challenges) harm the caster for 1 HP and the loss of an additional Soul point for 12 hours.


Equipment
"Equipment" refers to any tools that can be brought with the Animals as they move between pockets of civilization within the Subworld.


Characters
Characters are the primary agents through whom players interact with the world of the game.
