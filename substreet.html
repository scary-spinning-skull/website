SUBSTREET


What is this game?
Substreet is an amazing and totally unique game which is not a ripoff about rats, mice, and other animals who live in the sewers and tunnels of the Big City. Laboratory animals with heightened intelligence have been escaping from poorly designed enclosures for decades, and now vast swaths of the Understreet are populated by their descendants, who have built kingdoms and civilizations directly beneath the feet of the Humans who created them.

Kings and warlords vie for power, heroes keep the people safe from predators, and adventurers brave the terrors of the City to find rare (and valuable) treasures. And maybe, someday, they will rise up and claim a place of their own under the Sun...


How do you play?

Here, let me tell you...

Challenge Rolls
Players who wish for their character to perform an action that requires some degree of skill must make a challenge roll. Two pairs of ten-sided and six-sided dice are rolled. The six-sided dice (d6) represent the abilities of the character, while the ten-sided dice represent the difficulty of that specific task. If, after adding bonuses, the d6 results are higher than their accompanying d10s, the action is a success, but the reverse indicates a failure.

A mixed result, where one roll has a higher d6 outcome and the other has a higher d10, indicates an outcome where the intended action went off with a hitch that is determined by the DM (dungeon-sewer master"). The character jumps across a crevasse, but he drops a piece of equipment, for example. These results can be written as d6/d10-d6/d10: 5/4-6/3 for a success.

There are times when a Challenge will be unusually easy or difficult for a character to perform. When this happens, the player must roll the Challence dice once again. If the Challence is easier to perform, the player may count the highest two d6 rolls, and the lowest two if the Challenge is diffucult. These are referred to as "advantaged" and "disadvantaged" rolls.

For example: the character performing that jump is inebriated. The player makes a disadvantaged Challenge roll and gets: 2/9-6/2-2/7. Only the first and third rolls are taken into account, and so the rat fails the jump.

Sometimes the d6 and d10 will produce the same number on a Challenge roll. When that happens, the outcome of the other roll is checked. If that roll is a success, the action is critically successful. If that roll is a failure, the action is a critical failure. The rat soars over the chasm, or he misses the ledge, hits his head, and falls to the ground already unconscious.


Combat
Combat is always a Challenging endeavor which has unique modifiers. Attacks against a character who is under cover are Disadvantaged, as is any action performed immediately after a surprise encounter by both sides. If one side is surprised but not the other, the unsurprised side may act with Advantage on its first round.

A successful Challenge will normally deal one HP of damage, while a critical success will inflict two, while a failed Challenge results in a successful attack against the character. The blow from an attack may hit a character's armor, if he is wearing any (see armor below).


Psionics
Many of the lab animals who have had their intelligence heightened have developed psionic abilities which they wisely hid from the Humans who inculcated them in their subjects. Characters with a high Magus score (+2 or higher) may expend their Soul points to "cast spells", as the species of the Substreet think of it. A full list of these "spells" can be found here. While all of the spells are accessible to a novice, each level requires more Soul points: 1 point for level 1 spells, 2 points for level 2, and so on.
