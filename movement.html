Movement and Travel
While some movement in a game can be abstracted or waived, these are typically slower-paced social situations or those in which placement is irrelevant. If a mouse is quickly picking a lock while his salamander companions look over his shoulder, does it matter what order they're standing in?

However, sometimes events in the game narrative do necessitate some system for tracking location and placement. Who is where, who can hit who, can they or can they not flee? These are matters which can profoundly affect the outcome of any encounter.

Rules for movement are less strict than in most systems and are intended to allow a table to organize and complete a game encounter with minimal use of minis, grids, and other items which may make it seem to passers-by that you are playing with toys and not simulating combat. There's no pressure to use this particular system; the group may choose to alter it with "house rules," use an alternative system, or make up a new one entirely.

Large scale tactical combat is virtually unknown in the Understreet. Battles usually involve no more than a dozen combatants on either side, as there is simply not enough space within the sewers to maneuver, and activities aboveground are necessarily limited for fear of attracting Humans or other predators. If, however, such a battle were to take place during the course of your game, you may consult any of a great number of wargames which deal with warfare at platoon scale.

Movement
Distances in Substreet are broken up into three ranges: near (1"), middle (4"), and far (>4"). Near is striking distance, wherever a character can reach by swinging a sword. Things in the middle distance are out of reach of most handheld objects except for polearms, but it requires no special skill to hit a target there with a thrown item or a bow. Targets that are far away cannot be hit except by skilled Archers, Casters, and rockets.

If a group does decide to use an alternative system for engaging in combat, it should be remembered that mice are half the height (4'') of other intelligent Animals (8'' to 12'').


Travel
Except under special circumstances, all travel is by foot, since travel through the Substreet world entails vertical as well as horizontal movement. Carts and pack animals are not useful in these spaces.

Players may choose to hire non-player characters to help haul loot and equipment. Prices are negotiable, and some individuals may demand payment in barter rather than cash. Such characters do not fight and their Soul points are fixed at 3. A critical failure of their Courage means that they run away entirely and steal whatever they were carrying.

A normal speed for any group which is not stopping to rest of scavenge will move at around a half mile per hour. This is dangerous, however, as after the second hour of such travel the characters will begin to tire and accumulate disadvantages for Challenge rolls. A more normal movement speed, which takes into account the Animals' need for naps and scavenging for food, is a quarter mile an hour.

Animals are capable of short bursts of energy when fleeing an emeny, but this cannot be sustained for a great deal of time and will not affect movement on a large scale.
