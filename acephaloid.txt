Acephaloids are mutated blemmyes which live in forests, ruins, and any other place which can provide cover during the day. They are not intelligent enough to communicate with and are hostile to anyone outside of their species. Because they do not stop growing as they age, the few who do not succumb to starvation or predation by their peers become "daddies", huge and far more dangerous. They have the unique ability to let their eyes leave their bodies to operate independently, using their unusually long optic nerves as tentacles.

Sapient snakes: Init +3; Atk bite +1 (1d3), tiny axe (1d4) or tiny short sword (1d6); AC 18; HD 1d6; MV 20' (10' climbing); Act 1d20;  SP can manage 2 venomous bites per encounter, bitten targets must pass a DC 5 Fort save; Fort +0, Ref +6, Will +1; AL N.

Sapient snakes are a species discovered on Rasalhague II. Becoming pets is far more appealing to them than the stone age savagery of their homeworld, but spending months in a pet shop followed by years fetching drinks and performing tricks becomes tedious for many, and they tend to resent their owners if not properly cared for. Like terrestrial snakes, sapient snakes eat smaller animals whole; on Cretasus, they have learned to use carrion to attract edible scavengers, and some steal ornamental chickens to feast on their eggs.
