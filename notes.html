SOUL POINTS

Soul is an abstraction of a character's courage and their ability to "push through" and succeed despite the odds. Mechanically, Soul points are used in several ways:


  • Modifying the outcome of a Story die roll
  • Giving the character access to higher tiers of spells
  • Determining the ability of a character to overcome his fear

First, any Soul point can be spent on increasing the outcome of one Story die roll of the player's choice, but not both.


For example, a player announces that, given his character's +1 to Adept rolls, he is going to use one Soul point on his lowest roll to improve the outcome of a jump across a chasm. Including the +1 bonus, he rolls 2/5 (fail) and 7/8 (fail). The spent Soul point goes to the first roll and the rat falls to his death. If, however, he had rolled a 4/5 (fail) and 7/8 (fail) resulting in a Complication, his first roll would be raised to 5/5 and the leap would be accomplished, although he drops and loses a piece of equipment.


The player can also announce that he will give up one or more Soul points in anticipation of specific circulstances, such as a marginal failure (3/4, for example). If both rolls are successful, or the failures are too low to matter, the point is still lost.


Second, spending Soul points is necessary to access higher level spells. Level 1 spells are always available to characters, but the spending of a Soul point is necessary if a character is to use level 2 spells, and two are needed to use level 3 spells. These spent points to not go towards affecting the outcome of Story die rolls, but they are not counted when calculating the harmful effects ot a miscast spell, either (more on that in the SPELLS section).


Finally, Soul points are used when determining a character's reaction to frightful circumstances, specifically encounters with Giants, very large animals, and encounters in which half of a party have perished. To do this, the current Soul score is doubled, then checked against a d10 roll. If the result of that roll is less than the Soul score, the character's courage holds. If that roll is greater, their courage breaks and they flee. On a tie, the player may decide if the character remains frozen with fear, giving them another turn to muster their courage, or if they run away.


For example, say that a Mischief of three rats turns a corner in an alley and encounters a hungry Cat. Rat 1 has a current Soul score of 3, meaning that he has to roll below 6 with a d10 to keep his wits about him and deploy the can of mace he has strapped to his back. He succeeds: 6/4. Rat 2 is emotionally exhausted because he cast a very important spell earlier in the session, and his Soul has been reduced to 1. He fails his roll dramatically: 3/7. He runs away, while Rat 3, with 4 Soul points, rolls 8/8 and is rooted to the ground. On the next turn he rolls 8/4; Rat 2 shakes off his fear and joins Rat 1 in his fight with the now-blinded Cat.


Soul points can be gained and list in ways other than spending them on die rolls. They may be gained by the ingestion of certain potions which can be purchased or made by PCs with the Apothecary ability. Various concoctions exist, the cheapest being "herbal supplements", which only have a 50% chance of giving a character +1 to their Soul for the duration of the session. Potions made from Adderall, PCP, or other stimulants stolen from Giants are guaranteed to work, giving the character +2 to their Soul... but then cause a -1 penalty to their Soul on the next session, as their body is wracked with the pain of withdrawal and over-exertion. Herbal potions are a simple matter, but using Giant stimulants is a tricky matter and an Apothecary must be taught how to use them safely, or the potion they concoct has a 20% chance of harming a character for 1 hit point. If a chatacter has no Soul points remaining, the pain of withdrawal imposes a temporary -1 penalty to their Health instead.


Other ways to gain Soul points include: blessings from preists of a character's preferred deity, succeeding on a fully Hindered action without spending any Soul points, or succeeding at an unusually brave action (scaring away a Giant by oneself, for example). Soul points which were lost temporarily may be restored by 1 point for every 24 hours of in-game rest.


Characters gain one permanent increase in Soul points per level, and they also gain one permanent Soul point if they roll a critical success twice, even if a Soul point was used to raise the roll further, e.g. 5/5 and 7/7, with the Soul point raising the first roll to 6/5. A roll does not could as a doubly critical success if the Soul point were used to raise the roll, such as 4/5 -> 5/5.


ARMOR


Armor is any accoutrement which protects the wearer from damage. In Understreet, armor protects the wearer at six points: head, left arm, right arm, torso, legs, and tail. After a successful strike, the attacker rolls 1d10 to determine exactly where the target was hit. If that point is covered, the armor is damaged instead, becoming unusable when its health points are exhausted.


Strike Table
1 Head
2-4 Torso
5 Right arm
6 Left arm
7-8 Legs
9-10 Tail

Armor can be purchased from armorers, but it is far less expensive for characters to make it themselves from sacavenged materials, not to mention easier to acquire if you're not in a city! Armor can only be made for a specific race: mice can only wear mouse armor, rats can only wear rat armor, and salamanders can only wear salamander armor. The fabrication of armor entails the rolling of a Test roll:

Quality Table
Fail Defective
Complication Low quality, 1 HP
Success 2 HP
Success with double High quality, 3 HP


Characters who wear more than four pieces of armor may not apply expertise bonuses to any Adept rolls.

HEALING


After incurring injuries, there are several ways by which a character can heal:

  • Rest
  • Potions
  • Magic

Characters recover 1 health point for every 24 hours they spend resting. Combat, travel, or activities of any kind will prolong the recovery period.


For example, Bonescar the Rat incurred 2 HP of damage in a fight with a pigeon during an aboveground scavenging expedition. The nearest sewer grate is a quarter block away, too far for her in her condition. Fortunately, there is a hole in a nearby cinder block wall, and the space inside is large enough to hide out until she recovers. She manages to rest for a day, recovering 1 HP, but is forced to flee to a dumpster after spotting an alleycat and was unable to do any further healing until the end of the following day, at which point she was able to make a dash for the sewer.


Potions for healing are available to some characters. Apothecaries can manufacture healing potions from herbal supplements. It is only when ingesting the potion that its effectiveness is known. Complicated results heal 1 HP, but leaves the character woozy: Adept and Warrior bonuses are reduzed to 0 for one day. A success heals 1 point with no ill effects, and a critical success immediately brings the character to full health.


Spells are the last way to restore health.
