SOUL POINTS

Soul is an abstraction of a character's courage and their ability to "push through" and succeed despite the odds. Mechanically, Soul points are used in several ways:


  • Modifying the outcome of a Story die roll
  • Giving the character access to higher tiers of spells
  • Determining the ability of a character to overcome his fear

First, any Soul point can be spent on increasing the outcome of one Story die roll of the player's choice, but not both. For example, a player announces that, given his character's +1 to Adept rolls, he is going to use one Soul point on his lowest roll to improve the outcome of a jump across a chasm. Including the +1 bonus, he rolls 2/5 (fail) and 7/8 (fail). The spent Soul point goes to the 4/7 roll and the rat falls to his death. If, however, he had rolled a 6/5 (pass) and 7/8 (fail) resulting in a Complication, his first roll would be raised to 7/5 and there would be no difference in the outcome. The player can also announce that he will give up one Soul point in anticipation of a marginal failure (3/4, for example), but if both rolls are a success, or the failures are too low for it to matter, the point is still lost.


Second, spending Soul points is necessary to access higher level spells. Level 1 spells are always available to characters, but the spending of a Soul point is necessary if a character is to use level 2 spells, and two are needed to use level 3 spells. These spent points to not go towards affecting the outcome of Story die rolls, but they are not counted when calculating the harmful effects ot a miscast spell, either (more on that in the SPELLS section).


Finally, Soul points are used when determining a character's reaction to frightful circumstances, specifically encounters with Giants and encounters in which half of a party have perished. To do this, the current Soul score is doubled, then checked against a d10 roll. If the result of that roll is less than the Soul score, the character's courage holds. If that roll is greater, their courage breaks and they flee. On a tie, the player may decide if the character remains frozen with fear, giving them another turn to muster their courage, or if they run away.


For example, say that a Mischief of three rats turns a corner in an alley and encounters a hungry Cat. Rat 1 has a current Soul score of 3, meaning that he has to roll below 6 with a d10 to keep his wits about him and deploy the can of mace he has strapped to his back. He succeeds: 6/4. Rat 2 is emotionally exhausted because he cast a very important spell earlier in the session, and his Soul has been reduced to 1. He fails his roll dramatically: 2/7. He runs away, while Rat 3, with 4 Soul points, rolls 8/8 and is rooted to the ground. On the next turn he rolls 8/4, allowing him to gather his courage and join Rat 1 in his fight with the now-blinded cat.


Soul points can be gained and list in ways other than spending them on die rolls. They may be gained by the ingestion of certain potions which can be purchased or made by PCs with the Apothecary ability. Various concoctions exist, the cheapest being "herbal supplements", which only have a 50% chance of giving a character +1 to their Soul for the duration of the session. Potions made from Adderall, PCP, or other stimulants stolen from Giants are guaranteed to work, giving the character +2 to their Soul... but then cause a -1 penalty to their Soul on the next session, as their body is wracked with the pain of withdrawal and over-exertion. Herbal potions are a simple matter, but using Giant stimulants is a tricky matter and an Apothecary must be taught how to use them safely, or the potion they concoct has a 20% chance of harming a character for 1 hit point. If a chatacter has no Soul points remaining, the pain of withdrawal imposes a temporary -1 penalty to their Health instead.


Other ways to gain Soul points include: blessings from preists of a character's preferred deity, succeeding on a fully Hindered action without spending any Soul points, or succeeding at an unusually brave action (scaring away a Giant by oneself, for example). Soul points which were lost temporarily may be restored by 1 point for every 24 hours of in-game rest.


Characters gain one permanent increase in Soul points per level, and they also gain one permanent Soul point if they roll a critical success twice, even if a Soul point was used to raise the roll further, e.g. 5/5 and 7/7, with the Soul point raising the first roll to 6/5. A roll does not could as a doubly critical success if the Soul point were used to raise the roll, such as 4/5 -> 5/5.
